October 20, 1978

Dear Professor Aron

History and Theory, in collaboration with Wesleyan's Center for the Humanities is planning a small conference for next March, to discuss issues raised in and by Hayden White 's book Metahistory: The historical Imagination of Nineteenth century Europe. Although one sees this book increasingly cited in articles and other books, we believe that is has not received the thorough critical examination which it deserves. It is highly original in its application of literary history to the history of historiography , and radical in its reformulation of historical relativism and other classic issues.

The conference will be held in Middletownon March 16th and 17th, 1979 Main papare will be given by Maurice Mandelbaum (Philosophy, John Hopkins and Dartmouth , Hans Kellner (History, Michigan State), Paul Hernadi (Comparative litterature Iowa), and Edward Said (English, Columbia). There will be critics for each paper, and we plan to leave plenty of time for both formal and informal discussion. We plan to limit the conference to forty participants, from history, literary theory and philosophy.

We though that as a member of the Editorial Commitee you would like to know about the planning of this conference, and also to know that of course you are invited if you are interested, in participating. Since the budget for the conference is very low we can't offer to cover travel expanses, as we would like to do, but if you expect to be in the vicinity of Middletown next March in any case, by all means do let us know. We shall also be happy to send copies of the main papers, at your request. Naturally we hope that they will later be published in History and Theory.

Closing Remarks

Sincerely yours, Richard T Vann
